# Contact Section

## Purpose

A warm, low-pressure invitation to connect. The last impression the visitor takes with them.

This isn't a form — it's a doorway. It should feel like the end of a good conversation where someone says, "Let's keep talking."

## Tone

Warm. Direct. Not desperate.

```
❌ "Looking for my next opportunity! Let's connect!"
✅ "If something here resonated, I'd love to hear from you."
```

## Content

- **Headline**: Something genuine. Not "Get In Touch" (boring). More like "Let's build something together" or "Say hello."
- **Brief line**: A sentence that lowers the barrier. "Whether it's a role, a project, or just a thought — I'm always glad to hear from people who care about good work."
- **Links**: Email, GitHub, LinkedIn. Clear, not cluttered.
- **Optional**: A simple contact form (name, email, message). No backend needed in v1 — can use a mailto: link or a future integration.

## Layout Intent

- This section should feel like a gentle landing, not a hard stop
- Consider a visual callback to the hero — a return to the sky, a sunset gradient, a sense of the journey ending peacefully
- The contact info should be centered, spacious, and easy to find
- On mobile, everything stacks naturally

## Atmosphere

- Warmer tones here: sunset gradient, amber accents
- A sense of golden hour — the day wrapping up
- Optional: a small nature element — a distant bird, a setting sun silhouette, tall grass
- The transition from the previous section should feel like descending toward a warm horizon — a **scene transition** (see `system/animations.md` §2b) that gradually shifts the background from the Skills section's open-air feel into a warm sunset palette

## Interaction

- Contact links should have **generous hover states** — the link area expands with a warm glow or color shift on hover, making each one feel inviting rather than clinical
- On mobile, links should feel tappable with clear touch feedback (subtle scale or background flash)
- If a mailto: link is used, consider a brief **"copied to clipboard"** toast as an alternative interaction — some users prefer that over opening a mail client

## What to Avoid

- "Hire me!" energy
- Social media link dumps (keep it to 2–3 relevant platforms)
- Complex contact forms with required fields
- Calendar booking widgets (too transactional)
