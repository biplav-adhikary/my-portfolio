# Vision

## What This Is

A developer portfolio that feels like a quiet, well-crafted place — not a billboard.

Inspired by Studio Ghibli's visual sensibility: open skies, gentle motion, nature as a grounding metaphor. The site should feel like stepping into a calm scene and discovering someone's work and thinking at your own pace.

## Who It's For

**Primary:** Recruiters and hiring managers evaluating frontend candidates.
They scan dozens of portfolios. Most are forgettable. This one should make them slow down.

**Secondary:** Other engineers or collaborators who want to understand how I think — not just what tools I've used.

## What It Should Communicate

1. **Craft** — "This person cares about details and quality."
2. **Thinking** — "This person doesn't just code. They reason about problems."
3. **Taste** — "This person has opinions about design and UX, and they're good ones."
4. **Warmth** — "This person seems like someone I'd enjoy working with."

## What It Should NOT Be

- A resume in HTML form
- A grid of project thumbnails with no context
- A tech-stack bingo card
- Overly anime / illustrated to the point of being distracting
- So minimal that it says nothing

## The Feeling

Imagine walking through a quiet hillside at golden hour. The wind is gentle. There's a path ahead that you can follow or wander from. Things reveal themselves as you move. Nothing shouts.

That's the feeling.

## Success Criteria

- A recruiter spends more than 30 seconds on the site
- A visitor can articulate what I care about after 60 seconds
- The site itself is a portfolio piece — it demonstrates frontend craft just by existing
- It loads fast, works on mobile, and feels polished at every breakpoint
